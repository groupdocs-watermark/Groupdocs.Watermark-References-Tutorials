# Add Watermark to Document C# - Getting Started with GroupDocs.Watermark

Need to protect your documents, prove ownership, or add branding to PDFs, Word files, or images? You're in the right place. Adding watermarks programmatically might sound complex, but with GroupDocs.Watermark for .NET, you'll have your first watermarked document ready in about 15 minutes.

Whether you're building a document management system, protecting intellectual property, or just need to add "CONFIDENTIAL" stamps to sensitive files, this guide will walk you through everything you need to get started with document watermarking in C#. No prior watermarking experience required—just basic C# knowledge and you're good to go.

## Prerequisites: What You'll Need

Before diving into these tutorials, make sure you have:

That's it! You don't need any prior experience with watermarking libraries or document manipulation APIs.

## Common Use Cases for Document Watermarking

Still deciding if watermarking is right for your project? Here are real-world scenarios where developers implement GroupDocs.Watermark:

**Document Management Systems**: Automatically stamp "DRAFT" or "CONFIDENTIAL" on documents based on their status or classification level.

**Content Protection**: Add visible or invisible watermarks to PDFs or images to prove ownership and deter unauthorized distribution (think stock photos or white papers).

**Compliance Requirements**: Many industries require watermarked documents for audit trails. Healthcare, legal, and financial sectors often mandate visible tracking information.

**Branding and Marketing**: Add company logos or promotional text to documents sent to clients—every PDF becomes a subtle marketing opportunity.

**Version Control**: Timestamp watermarks help teams identify document versions at a glance, reducing confusion about which file is current.

**Security Layers**: Combine visible "SAMPLE" watermarks with invisible digital signatures for multi-layered document protection.
